From Neurons to Society: A Multiscale Approach

From Neurons to Society: A Multiscale Approach to Digital Neuroscience A defining feature of this program is its multiscale approach to understanding cognition, behavior, and neurotechnology, spanning from single-neuron recordings to large-scale social systems. Neuron Level: The program incorporates in vivo and in vitro single-neuron recordings, computational models of neural circuits, and brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) to understand how fundamental neural processes give rise to cognition and perception. Systems Level: Students gain expertise in EEG, fMRI, biosensors, and multimodal neural data analysis to explore brain dynamics in cognitive states, decision-making, and learning. Behavioral Level: Eye-tracking, motion capture, and human-computer interaction methods allow students to study real-time neurobehavioral adaptation and cognitive workload in applied environments such as education, aviation, and high-performance sports. Social Level: The program extends to hyperscanning and collective intelligence models, analyzing how groups of individuals collaborate, innovate, and make decisions in complex environments like digital workspaces, smart cities, and policy design. This multilevel analysis enables graduates to translate neuroscientific knowledge into real-world applications, whether in precision healthcare, adaptive learning technologies, or social computing (MIT Technology Review, 2023).

        <li><strong>Master Thesis (60 ECTS):</strong> A substantial research project conducted under the supervision of our esteemed faculty or industry partners, culminating in a written thesis and formal presentation.</li>

      <h2>Core Teaching Units (41 ECTS)</h2>

      <p>

        Core courses include an introduction to neuroscience, foundations of informatics, current topics in digital neuroscience, and practical training in Python programming, cybernetic systems, advanced EEG workshops, and health data analysis from wearable biosensors. These courses are designed to build a robust foundation in both neuroscience and digital methodologies.

      </p>


      <h2>Elective Teaching Units (19 ECTS)</h2>

      <p>

        Electives allow you to further specialize in areas such as neuroethics, clinical neuroscience, behavioral methods, user-centered design, pattern recognition, distributed algorithms, and big data infrastructures. You have the flexibility to choose courses that align with your career aspirations and research interests.

      </p>


      <h2>Master Thesis (60 ECTS)</h2>

      <p>

        In the final phase, you will undertake a comprehensive research project that integrates the skills and knowledge acquired during the program. You will work closely with an experienced researcher, either in our labs or through external collaborations, to produce a thesis that contributes to the field of digital neuroscience.

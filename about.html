From Neurons to Society: A Multiscale Approach

A defining feature of this program is its multiscale approach to understanding cognition, behavior, and neurotechnology, spanning from single-neuron recordings to large-scale social systems.

Neuron Level

- Involves in vivo and in vitro single-neuron recordings.
- Uses computational models of neural circuits.
- Explores brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) for cognition and perception.

Systems Level

- Focuses on EEG, fMRI, biosensors, and multimodal data analysis.
- Investigates brain dynamics in cognitive states, decision-making, and learning.

Behavioral Level

- Uses eye-tracking, motion capture, and human-computer interaction.
- Analyzes real-time neurobehavioral adaptation and cognitive workload.
- Applied in education, aviation, and sports.

Social Level

- Uses hyperscanning and collective intelligence models.
- Studies collaboration, innovation, and decision-making in smart cities and policy design.
